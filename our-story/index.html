Our Story

Why I Built Unlock Patients

It started with my mom's pediatric practice in Nashville and one question nobody could answer: where are your patients actually coming from?

Growing Up in a Practice

My mom's a pediatrician. She's been running her own practice in Nashville my entire life. I watched her pour everything into her patients — early mornings, late nights, weekends on call. She's incredible at what she does.

But there was one thing she could never crack: getting new families through the door consistently. She tried agencies. They'd send flashy reports full of impressions and clicks, charge a few thousand a month, and when she'd ask "how many new patients did I actually get from this?" — nobody could tell her.

"She was writing checks on faith. I wanted to see if I could actually prove it — not vanity metrics, but this person saw this ad, called this number, and booked an appointment."
Alex Langone, CEO & Founder

So I Built It Myself

I started with her practice as the guinea pig. Set up the Google Ads campaigns, built a landing page, wired up call tracking with dedicated phone numbers so we'd know exactly which calls came from which ads.

Within a few months, her schedule was filling up. But the real thing was that for the first time, she could see exactly where every new patient came from — which campaign, which keyword, which ad. No one had ever given her that before.

Every Practice Has This Problem

My mom isn't unique. Every pediatric practice owner I've talked to has the same story — they're amazing doctors who got into medicine to help kids, not to become digital marketing experts. They're either overpaying agencies that can't prove results, or they're just not doing any marketing at all.

So I built the whole system from scratch. The ad campaigns, the landing pages, the call tracking, the real-time dashboard — all on infrastructure we own. No middlemen, no markup, no vanity metrics. When I say a campaign brought in 47 new patients last month, I can show you every single one of them.

We're still a small team. Every practice gets the same hands-on attention I gave my mom's practice from day one. That's not changing.
